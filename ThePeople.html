Internet: People Power

  • The Web has changed the world and People are changing the Web. People have more power through the music industry, social news websites, television broadcasting sites, and more. Blogging became popular. People were posting thoughts, expertise and everyone could be a journalist.
  • 1995 - Craigslist was founded by Craig Newmark. He ran his website out of his apartment; It started expanding to other U.S. and Canadian cities in 2000.
  • June 1999 - Napster was released and within a week, thousands of people had downloaded it. Napster was a dream for music lovers. It introduced music through computers & internet; MP3 files – made it easier to compress data; It was founded by Sean Fanning. Napster was a program that listed all the music files and shared and played with anyone else who used the software. In less than 4 months, Napster reached the million download mark and had become the fastest spreading software. Hillary Rosen, the head of the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) called up fanning and told him it was illegal.
  • May 3, 2000 – Metallica’s drummer came to Napster in a fury about the illegal music file sharing. They came to Napster’s small office to find it was just a couple of guys in a small office in a bank building with 250 reporters that were waiting outside.
  • 2000 - Metallica and other music companies through the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) lead by Hillary Rosen filed a lawsuit against Napster.
  • July 2001 - The legal battle with Napster ended with a verdict that Napster had to be shut down immediately; The Music industry shut down Napster; This encouraged Steve Jobs and Apple to create the Itunes music store for which songs were sold for 99 cents
  • 2003- MySpace was founded by Tom Anderson & Chris DeWolfe. It attracted 27 Million registered users; It initially got popular with sharing music.
  • 2004- Digg founded by Kevin Rose & Jay Adelson was launched. It was a social news website that allowed people to vote their stories into the news. The more people that voted for a story, that story would get on the site.
  • 2004- Marc Zuckerberg, Founder & CEO of Facebook. Two thirds of Harvard students were using Facebook within a week; Then other schools reached out and it expanded to other colleges, highschools and companies; Zuckerberg created a service that had the ultimate relationship engine. All the relationships that people, the social graph, was mapped out. It showed that somehow everyone is connected.With four days of launching it, it turned into the Window of the Web.
  • 2004 - Web 2.0 was founded by Tim O Reilly.
  • 2005 - MySpace was everywhere; Rupert Murdoch wrote a big check to take over my space; He bought it for $650 million.
  • 2005 - YouTube was founded by Chad Hurley. YouTube has changed television control- Broadcast Yourself and was the best known startup.
  • 2006 - Google bought out Youtube for 1.65 billion dollars
  • Viacom launched a 1 billion dollar lawsuit against Youtube due to copyrighted material that appears on the website; Youtube was under Google so that helped protect them; Youtube helped bring people into power by allowing them to post their videos to the public. It changed the way of the TV world.
  • Jimmy Wales, the founder of Wikipedia stated, “The Web belongs to us. The Web is turning into the medium of the little guy. People can write a blog and wiki.”
  • Tim Berners-Lee, the Inventor of the World Wide Web said that through the Web, we bring back the common person as the author.
